Inside 10 Stories
04·26
This week the industry crossed a quiet line: AI is no longer a feature in your tech stack — it is the stack.
Three forces collided this week. RIA M&A hit a record opening quarter, with average deal size now north of $1.2 billion and consolidators paying up for scale. AI moved from sidecar to operating system, with Citi launching a client-facing avatar, Wealth.com raising $65 million, and at least one upstart announcing it will eliminate its advisor workforce entirely. And Washington queued up a regulatory month that will reshape onboarding, custody, and what you put in 401(k)s. The advisors who win the next twelve months are the ones who turn this noise into clear client conversations now — before the rules, the buyers, and the bots get there first.
Industry M&A & Recruiting
01 M&A
LPL and Private Advisor Group buy Mariner Advisor Network — 367 advisors, $31B in assets
Financial Planning · Industry M&A
The largest advisor-network transaction in recent memory hands LPL the affiliation chassis Mariner had spent years building. It is also a tell: the breakaway and tuck-in market is now so attractive that LPL is willing to pay for the funnel, not just the firms inside it. Expect more recruiting platforms to be packaged and sold as standalone businesses.
Content Angle
Write a client note titled "Why Your Advisor's Firm Just Got Bigger (And Why That Matters to You)" — explain consolidation in plain language and reassure clients that their plan, not the parent company, is what governs outcomes.
02 M&A Data
Average RIA deal size surges to $1.2 billion in early 2026 — 93 deals in Q1 ties the record
The Daily Upside · Industry M&A
Average AUM per acquisition has climbed from $819 million in 2023 to nearly $1.2 billion this quarter. Translation: buyers are skipping the small shops and writing larger checks for established firms with clean data, niche expertise, and next-gen depth. If you're a $200M shop hoping to sell at the top, the ladder just got pulled up.
Content Angle
Publish a short LinkedIn post: "Three things buyers will pay for in 2026 (and three things they won't)." Use it to start succession conversations with founders in your network.
03 Super-Ensemble
Hightower's Signature Wealth adds a $3.2B Massachusetts super-ensemble as breakaways accelerate
WealthManagement.com · Recruiting
The wirehouse-to-RIA migration that pundits have called "imminent" for a decade is now mechanical. Independent channels are growing headcount at roughly 5% annually while wirehouses shrink at 1%. Hightower's super-ensemble model — large multi-advisor teams operating under a unified brand — is becoming the dominant landing pad for breakaways with $1B+ books.
Content Angle
Write a tear sheet: "What Independence Actually Buys You — A Breakaway's Real Math." Useful for recruiting other advisors and for clients who quietly wonder why so many of their friends' advisors keep changing logos.
Technology & AI
04 AI / Client UX
Citi unveils Google-powered AI avatar "Citi Sky" — and a note-taker called CitiScribe
WealthManagement.com · Wealthtech
Citi just shipped the first credible client-facing AI avatar from a global wealth manager. Citigold clients get conversational market and planning guidance through a Google DeepMind-powered persona this summer. Meanwhile CitiScribe — an AI meeting note-taker — is already deployed across all North American Citi advisors. The arms race for the client's attention now includes a non-human counterpart, and your clients will start asking why your firm doesn't have one.
Content Angle
Send a client email titled "When Your Bank's Chatbot Knows More Than Your Advisor" — explain the difference between an AI that answers questions and a human who designs your plan, then invite a conversation.
05 WealthTech Funding
Wealth.com closes $65M Series B for AI estate and tax platform — claims firms managing $15T use it
WealthManagement.com · Funding
Estate planning has been the slowest-moving piece of the advisor stack for thirty years. That is changing fast. Wealth.com's raise validates a thesis the industry has been circling for two years: AI is the only viable way to scale estate review, beneficiary tracking, and document drafting across thousands of households. If you're not running annual estate audits, your competitors will be — automatically.
Content Angle
Build a one-page checklist: "10 Things Your Estate Plan Should Be Reviewed For Every Year." Send it to every household over $1M in net worth on your book.
06 AdvisorTech
Kitces' April AdvisorTech roundup: AI agents land in Wealthbox, Jump, RightCapital, Wealthstream
Kitces.com · AdvisorTech
Michael Kitces' monthly map confirms what every demo this spring has signaled: agentic AI — software that doesn't just suggest, but acts — is now the default product roadmap across CRM, planning, and meeting tools. Range is openly planning to eliminate its advisor workforce. The honest question for every firm right now is no longer "should we adopt AI" but "which workflows do we hand over first, and to whom."
Content Angle
Write a piece called "What I'm Letting AI Do — and What I'm Not." Be specific. Clients want to know whether their advisor is using these tools well or hiding from them.
Regulatory & Compliance
07 SEC
SEC's mega-month: Customer ID rule, custody amendments, and a crypto framework all queued for April
Plante Moran / SEC · Regulatory
The Customer Identification Program rule is heading for final action this month, custody rule amendments are slated for proposed rulemaking, and a crypto investment-product framework is on the same calendar. RIAs of every size will need new onboarding workflows, identity verification vendors, and updated written supervisory procedures within the next two quarters. If your compliance team has not seen the calendar yet, this is your nudge.
Content Angle
Send a one-page advisor-to-client memo: "Why You'll See New Forms From Us This Year — And What's Behind Them." Position the new rules as a benefit, not a burden.
08 Cybersecurity
Mercer Advisors hit by ShinyHunters — class-action filed as Reg S-P deadline looms for smaller firms
Financial Planning · Cybersecurity
Mercer was given 48 hours to pay or watch 5.7 million client records hit the dark web. A class-action followed days later. With Reg S-P's smaller-entity compliance deadline arriving June 3, this case will be a template for how plaintiffs argue "reasonable safeguards." Expect every RIA cyber policy to be re-priced this summer, and expect clients to start asking pointed questions.
Content Angle
Publish "Five Questions to Ask Your Advisor About Their Cybersecurity." Beat the breach headlines to your clients with a proactive, plain-English explanation.
Tax, Retirement & Planning
09 Retirement / DOL
DOL proposes landmark rule to put alternative investments in 401(k) plans
U.S. Department of Labor · Retirement
The proposed rule gives plan fiduciaries an actual framework — performance, fees, liquidity, valuation, complexity — for evaluating private equity, private credit, and real assets inside defined contribution plans. This will not happen overnight, but it will reshape what "diversified" means inside a 401(k) over the next decade. Plan-advisor practices in particular should be on this now.
Content Angle
For plan-sponsor clients, draft a memo: "What the DOL's New Alternatives Rule Could Mean for Your Plan." For retail clients, write "Private Markets Are Coming to Your 401(k) — Here's What to Know."
10 Estate & Tax
The OBBBA reset: $15M estate exemption is now permanent — and most clients' plans assume the old sunset
Mercer Advisors / PwC · Tax Planning
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act locked the unified estate and gift exemption at $15M per person ($30M per couple), permanently indexed for inflation. Annual exclusion is $19,000 per recipient. The trap: nearly every estate plan written between 2020 and 2024 was built around the now-defunct sunset and is structurally outdated. Trusts are mistuned, gifts are misallocated, and step-up calculations are wrong.
Content Angle
Run a 30-day campaign — a client letter, a webinar, and a one-on-one outreach list — built around the prompt "Has your estate plan been re-read since the law changed?" This is the single highest-conversion content topic in your library this year.
