05·01

AI is rewriting the advisor desktop while consolidation hits a new high — and Powell takes one last bow.

Three forces are colliding this week and every one of them lands on your desk. AI is no longer a back-office assistant — it is restructuring the advisor stack itself, with note-takers becoming operating systems and one RIA publicly betting it can run with almost no human advisors at all. Meanwhile LPL just bought $31 billion of Mariner's advisor network, the Fed held rates in Powell's swan-song meeting, and the SEC keeps lining up its 2026 enforcement targets around testimonials and undisclosed conflicts. If you are not having pointed conversations with clients about both the Fed pause and the new permanent $15 million estate exclusion, your competitor is.

Industry M&A

01 Industry M&A

LPL Buys Mariner's $31B Advisor Network in Year's Biggest Deal

AdvisorHub

LPL and its affiliate Private Advisor Group are absorbing 367 advisors and $31 billion in client assets from Mariner Advisor Network, the largest transaction of an already record-setting year. 223 advisors stay direct with LPL while 144 move to PAG's hybrid RIA. The signal is consolidation is no longer a story about small tuck-ins — the platforms are eating each other's affiliate channels in nine and ten-figure chunks.

Content Angle

Write a client note titled 'Why your advisor's firm name might change — and what it does not change for you.' Position your independence as the reason your client experience is insulated from this kind of disruption.

02 Industry M&A

Hightower Plans More 'Flagship' RIAs After Bahnsen Group Acquisition

WealthManagement.com

Hightower is signaling more large-firm acquisitions modeled on its Bahnsen Group deal, with the new Hightower Signature Wealth channel already past $29 billion. The Bahnsen template — buy a brand-name firm and let it operate semi-autonomously — is now a playbook other aggregators will copy. Expect founder-led RIAs in the $1B–$5B band to receive more inbound calls than ever this year.

Content Angle

Publish a piece on what makes a firm acquirable in 2026 — process documentation, team continuity, and clean compliance — even for advisors who have no intention of selling. It is the same checklist that builds enterprise value.

Technology / AI

03 Technology / AI

FINNY Launches Hunter, an AI Engine That Runs Advisor Marketing

WealthManagement.com

FINNY's new Hunter tool, fresh off a $17M Series A, automates marketing strategy, content creation, and campaign execution for advisors. It is an explicit move from tool to agent — software that operates on the advisor's behalf rather than waiting for the advisor to operate it. The interesting question is not whether it works in April, it is what your COI referrals look like when half your peers are publishing machine-generated thought leadership at scale.

Content Angle

Audit your own content this month. If everything you publish could plausibly have been written by an AI agent in fifteen seconds, you have a positioning problem, not a marketing problem.

04 Technology / AI

The Latest in Financial AdvisorTech (April 2026): The Stack Is Becoming an OS

Kitces.com

Kitces' April roundup is the clearest map yet of what is actually happening: Wealthbox is bolting AI agents onto the CRM, Jump and Zocks are morphing from note-takers into full advisor operating systems, RightCapital is parsing client documents automatically, and Morningstar is unloading ByAllAccounts. Translation: the AI integration layer is the new battleground, and your CRM is no longer the center of the desktop.

Content Angle

Run a 30-day test of one AI note-taker that integrates with your CRM. Write up what changed in your post-meeting workflow. That post will outperform any generic 'AI is coming for advisors' piece by 10x.

05 Technology / AI

Range Says It Will Eliminate Most Human Advisors Within Three Years

The Daily Upside

Range, an RIA managing $700 million, has said publicly it will replace most of its human advisor workforce with AI inside three years. Whether that is real, theatre, or a recruiting lever does not matter — the press release is now in every prospect's search results. Have an answer ready when a client asks why they are paying you when an algorithm down the road is a tenth the price.

Content Angle

Draft a one-page 'why a human advisor' document — not defensive, not nostalgic. Specific examples of decisions you have helped clients make in the last 12 months that no model could have made for them.

Markets & Fed

06 Markets & Fed

Fed Holds at 3.50–3.75% in Powell's Final Meeting; Warsh Up Next

CNBC

The FOMC voted 11-1 to hold rates steady, citing the inflation tick-up and Iran uncertainty. It was Powell's last meeting; Kevin Warsh takes the chair next month. JPMorgan Research now sees no cuts in 2026 and the next move as a 25 bp hike in Q3 2027. Reset client expectations now — the easy-money narrative is over for the foreseeable future and rate-sensitive allocations need a deliberate review.

Content Angle

Send a one-paragraph client note this week: what the pause means for cash, intermediate bonds, and any dividend equity tilt. Lead with what does not change before what does.

Regulatory / Compliance

07 Regulatory / Compliance

SEC Marketing Rule Enforcement in 2026 Targets Testimonials and Endorsements

Mintz

The December 2025 Risk Alert telegraphed exactly what is coming: testimonials, endorsements, and third-party ratings will drive the next enforcement wave, with an explicit warning that repeat findings get referred to Enforcement. Add the Vanguard Advisers conflict-of-interest case and the FY25 'fraud-first' framing and the picture is clear — the staff is hunting undisclosed compensation and incentives, not paperwork misses. Buyers, breakaways, and growth-mode RIAs are all squarely in scope.

Content Angle

Pull every testimonial, Google review, and Wealthtender-style rating you display and check three things: client status, compensation, and material conflict — each disclosed clear and prominent, not buried.

Cybersecurity

08 Cybersecurity

LPL and Mercer Both Hit by Breaches in the Same Week

Securities Lawyers Blog

Phishing-driven malware compromised individual advisor devices at LPL, then jumped to the web portal — 1,581 clients affected, with unauthorized trades and transfers actually executed. Days later Mercer Advisors disclosed its own incident. The lesson is the obvious one nobody acts on until it happens: the weakest link is the advisor's laptop, not the custodian's vault.

Content Angle

Send a proactive 'how we protect your data' email this month. Cover MFA, device policy, what an LPL-style breach would and would not affect, and what your client should do if they ever get a request to wire from your address that feels off.

Markets & Client Communication

09 Markets & Client Communication

Fidelity Rolls Out Private Markets Model Portfolios for Advisors

InvestmentNews

Fidelity is putting private equity, private credit, and real assets inside turnkey models for the advisor channel, with paired education content. Combined with the growing list of evergreen private credit ETFs and CNBC's recent piece on private credit fears bleeding into fixed-income ETFs, the takeaway is the same: alts are now an allocation conversation, not a niche conversation. Have your written view before clients ask.

Content Angle

Write a 400-word piece called 'Three honest questions to ask before your portfolio owns private credit' — liquidity, valuation, and what happens in a real default cycle. Honest, not hype.

Practice Management

10 Practice Management

Consultants Warn AI Could Drive the Next Wave of Advisor Fee Compression

AdvisorHub

Cerulli has the average AUM fee on a $10 million relationship slipping to 66 bps by 2026, and consultants now expect AI to accelerate the slide for advisors who anchor their value on planning and portfolio analysis. Combine that with 40% of advisors over 55 and only 6% of founders with a documented succession plan and you have the next five years of practice-management headlines. The advisors who win will sell something a model cannot price-anchor — judgment, coordination, and behavior.

Content Angle

Audit your own fee schedule and what each tier actually delivers. If you cannot name three things at your top tier that are genuinely outside an algorithm's reach, build them this quarter.
